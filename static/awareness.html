Immediate Response

If You Have Just Been Targeted

If you believe you have been the target of a fraud attempt — whether money was transferred or not — the steps below should be taken as quickly as possible.

🛡️
Immediate Action Checklist
Follow these steps in order. Stay calm — rapid action matters most.
1
Stay calm and do not panic. Acting hastily can make the situation worse. Take a moment to collect information before reacting.
2
Contact your bank immediately using the official helpline number listed on the back of your debit/credit card or the bank's official website. Request a freeze on suspicious transactions.
3
Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (available 24×7 in India). This is the fastest route to freeze fraudulent transactions through the banking network.
4
File a formal complaint at the official portal: cybercrime.gov.in. Have your details ready: transaction ID, amount, date, and the suspected fraudster's contact.
5
Preserve all evidence — take screenshots of messages, note down phone numbers, transaction IDs, email addresses, and chat logs before blocking the sender.
💡 Why speed matters: Quick reporting significantly increases the chances of freezing fraudulent transactions before funds are withdrawn or transferred further. The 1930 helpline is directly linked to the banking system for rapid intervention.
Category Breakdown

Scam-Specific Protection Guide

Each scam type uses different tactics. Understanding the specific pattern helps you respond correctly and avoid escalating the damage.

Fraudulent UPI payment requests, deceptive QR codes that debit rather than credit your account, and fake bank customer care representatives asking for OTP or remote access to your device.
⚡ Immediate Actions
  • Do not approve any pending UPI payment request.
  • Do not share OTP or UPI PIN with anyone — ever.
  • Open your bank app and block UPI transactions immediately.
  • Contact your bank's official customer care.
  • Call 1930 immediately if money has already been transferred.
📋 Follow-Up Steps
  • File a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.
  • Inform your bank branch in writing with full transaction details.
  • Change your UPI PIN and banking password.
  • Enable transaction SMS alerts if not already active.
🛡️ Prevention
  • Scanning a QR code to receive money is never required — it is always a scam.
  • Always call bank numbers listed on the official bank website, not numbers from SMS or Google.
  • Enable UPI transaction limits in your bank app.
  • Never grant screen-sharing access during a "customer care" call.
Messages or calls informing you that you have won a prize, lottery, or government scheme — but require an upfront "processing fee," "tax payment," or personal documents to release the winnings.
⚡ Immediate Actions
  • Do not pay any processing fee, tax, or "release charge."
  • Do not share copies of Aadhaar, PAN, or passport.
  • Block the sender on all platforms.
  • If money was already sent, call 1930 immediately.
📋 Follow-Up Steps
  • Report the number/account at cybercrime.gov.in.
  • Alert family members, especially elderly relatives who may be re-targeted.
  • If documents were shared, consider applying for a new ID and informing UIDAI.
🛡️ Prevention
  • Legitimate lotteries you have entered never demand upfront payment to release winnings.
  • You cannot win a lottery you did not enter. Unsolicited wins are always fraudulent.
  • Verify any "government scheme" through the official government portal directly.
Fraudulent job offers — often via WhatsApp or SMS — that promise unrealistic salaries or work-from-home income, then ask for a registration fee, security deposit, or training cost.
⚡ Immediate Actions
  • Stop all communication with the recruiter immediately.
  • Do not transfer any registration, training, or security fee.
  • Verify the company via its official website and only contact through the domain email.
  • If money was already sent — call 1930 and contact your bank immediately.
📋 Follow-Up Steps
  • Preserve all communication records as evidence.
  • File a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.
  • Report the job listing on the platform where you found it (LinkedIn, job boards, WhatsApp).
🛡️ Prevention
  • Genuine employers do not charge any fee at any stage of the hiring process.
  • Be sceptical of offers with unrealistic salaries and no interview process.
  • Verify job postings directly on the company's official career page.
  • Unsolicited job offers via WhatsApp from unknown numbers are almost always fraudulent.
Schemes promising guaranteed high returns — often via cryptocurrency, stock tips, or investment apps — that eventually disappear with deposited funds. Often use celebrity or authority endorsements.
⚡ Immediate Actions
  • Stop all further payments or deposits immediately.
  • Do not transfer more funds to "unlock" previous investments.
  • Save all receipts, screenshots, and transaction records.
  • Contact your bank to dispute or freeze associated transactions.
📋 Follow-Up Steps
  • File a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.
  • For securities or market-related fraud, report to SEBI (sebi.gov.in).
  • File a formal police complaint (FIR) at your nearest cybercrime police station.
🛡️ Prevention
  • No legitimate investment guarantees fixed high returns. If it sounds too good to be true, it is.
  • Verify that any financial advisor or firm is registered with SEBI before investing.
  • Be very cautious of celebrity-endorsed crypto or trading schemes on social media.
  • Check RBI's approved list of financial entities before engaging any new platform.
Fraudsters impersonate banks, RBI, or telecom operators via SMS, WhatsApp, or calls — claiming your KYC is expired and your account will be blocked unless you click a link or share details urgently.
⚡ Immediate Actions
  • Do not click links in unsolicited SMS or WhatsApp messages.
  • Navigate to your bank's website manually by typing the URL directly.
  • Contact your bank using the number on your card or official website.
  • Do not share OTP, CVV, or PIN with anyone — banks never ask for this.
📋 If Details Were Shared
  • Change all banking passwords and PINs immediately.
  • Block your debit and credit cards and request replacements.
  • Enable all available SMS and email transaction alerts.
  • File a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.
🛡️ Prevention
  • Banks and RBI never request OTP, PIN, or password over call, SMS, or WhatsApp.
  • Legitimate KYC updates happen at the branch or through the official app — never via a link.
  • Verify a website's URL carefully — scammers use near-identical domain names (e.g. sbi-kyc.com vs sbi.co.in).
  • Use an ad-blocker and keep your browser updated to reduce exposure to phishing pages.
Official Channels

Official Reporting & Regulatory Resources

Use only these verified official channels to report fraud and seek assistance. Avoid third-party "recovery" services — many are themselves fraudulent.

🌐
National Cyber Crime Portal
India's primary portal for reporting all digital fraud and cybercrime. Operated by the Ministry of Home Affairs.
cybercrime.gov.in → 📞 Helpline: 1930 (24×7)
🏦
Reserve Bank of India (RBI)
For banking fraud, loan-related scams, and unauthorised payment operator complaints. Check RBI's approved entity list before engaging any financial service.
rbi.org.in → sachet.rbi.org.in (Alert Portal) →
📊
SEBI — Securities & Investment Fraud
For investment scams, fake trading platforms, unregistered advisors, and stock market fraud. Verify advisor registrations here before investing.
sebi.gov.in → SCORES Complaint Portal →
🏛️
Your Bank — Official Contact
Always use the helpline number printed on the back of your bank card or listed on the official bank website. Never use numbers from SMS, WhatsApp, or Google Search results.
Use number on your card or official bank website only.
